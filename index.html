Mental Health Awareness

Having a Mental Health condition is not anything to be ashamed of. An estimated 26% of Americans ages 18 and older -- about 1 in 4 adults -- suffers from a diagnosable mental disorder in a given year. Estimates suggest that only half of people with mental illnesses receive treatment.

According to the World Health Organization, anxiety and depressive disorders are the most common mental health disorders, affecting 970 million people worldwide in 2019.

In the United States, some of the most common mental health disorders include:

  • Major depression
    • Also called clinical depression, this mood disorder affects about 1 in 4 adults in the U.S. each year. Symptoms include a lowered mood, loss of interest and enjoyment, and reduced energy.
  • Manic depression
    • Also called bipolar disorder, this mood disorder affects about 2.6% of Americans age 18 and older each year.
  • Schizophrenia
    • A complex psychotic disorder that affects about 1% of Americans, characterized by disruptions to thinking and emotions, and a distorted perception of reality.
  • Post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD)
    • About 15 million adults have PTSD, which can develop after experiencing trauma. Symptoms include flashbacks, nightmares, difficulty concentrating and sleeping, agitation, restlessness, and avoiding reminders of the trauma.

Other common mental health disorders include:

  • Generalized anxiety disorder
  • Panic disorder
  • Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD)
  • Disruptive behavior disorders, such as oppositional defiant disorder and conduct disorder
  • Eating disorders

How Can I Help?


  • Listen. Simply giving someone the space to talk can be really helpful in itself.
  • Offer reassurance..Seeking help can be scary sometimes.
  • Be Patient
  • Try not to make assumptions. What works for one may not be the answer for another.

  • Mental illness is NOT your fault, but a medical condition and a product of your biology and, therefore, not the result of one or multiple life events (although major life events can trigger them).
  • Seeking help is NOT a sign of weakness, but strength.
  • Mental illness is very hard to identify. It may seem that the person is perfectly fine and full of enthusiasm from the outside. On the inside, however, they may suffer unimaginable mental suffering.

  • Openly discussing mental health issues creates awareness.
  • Connect with other people. Good relationships are important for your mental wellbeing.
  • Be physically active. Being active is not only great for your physical health and fitness.
  • Promote Self-Care Activities: Encourage self-care practices such as meditation sessions, yoga classes, art therapy, or nature walks.
